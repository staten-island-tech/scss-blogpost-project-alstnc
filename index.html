first of all, what is vaporwave?

vaporwave is an electronic musical subgenre that first appeared in the early 2010s. vaporwave emerged in response to the immense economic and social forces that are still very much part of our lives: globalization, consumerism, and the key nostalgia generated among them. the sound can be described as slowed-down, chopped samples of smooth jazz, elevator, r&b, and lounge music from the 1980s and 90s. although its original goal was to function as a meme, its influence and impact reached well beyond its intended roots.

vaporwave as an aesthetic

heavily characterized by its aesthetic, vaporwave is known for its glitch art graphics, 80s and 90s internet-inspired imagery packed with renaissance-era statues. it implements cyberpunk/retro imagery, Japanese characters, plants (specifically, aloe, ferns, and palms), sad faces, ancient internet age emoticons, the colours pink and turquoise, water imagery, and, of course, gifs of those big, bulky computers from when dial-up was still a thing
